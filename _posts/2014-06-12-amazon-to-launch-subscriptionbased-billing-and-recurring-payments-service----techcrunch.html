Amazon is introducing a new payments service today, which the company hopes will soon be adopted by startup companies and others offering subscription-based or recurring payments, according to a report from Reuters out this morning, and Amazon now confirms.The service will allow these businesses to tap into Amazon’s over 240 million monthly active users who currently store their credit card information on Amazon.com, then pay for things ranging from music subscriptions to monthly phone bills.The report indicated that the service has already been in testing with a number of startup companies over the last few months, including mobile phone company Ting, owned by Tucows.And PeachDish.
