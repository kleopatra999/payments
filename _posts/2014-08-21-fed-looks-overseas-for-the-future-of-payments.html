The payments infrastructure in the United States is painfully out of date — and painfully slow.Newly updated payments systems in other countries are helping the Fed figure out a strategy.Sean Rodriguez, senior vice president of payment industry relations at the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago, told Bank Innovation that the challenges with the U.S.payments infrastructure may be unique, but that there is still much to be learned from what other countries have accomplished.
