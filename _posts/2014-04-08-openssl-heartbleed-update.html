The internet was recently rocked by the announcement of a critical vulnerability in OpenSSL dubbed Heartbleed.Libraries provided by OpenSSL are used by approximately two thirds of companies on the internet to secure their communication.Companies across the internet are now working to both patch the vulnerability as well as take appropriate precautions against any potential exploits of the vulnerability.This vulnerability has been given the CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) identifier of CVE–2014–0160.Braintree was using a version of OpenSSL that was vulnerable to Heartbleed at the time of the announcement.
